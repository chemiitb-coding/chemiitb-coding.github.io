Boost up your Coding Immunity with a Coder Community!

Communities

Coders together strong!

The role of an effective, vibrant and helpful community can just never be ignored when it comes to a coding newbie learning everything from scratch. The moment you step in this world, everything starts to seem highly demotivating if you are not surrounded with people whom you can approach without any hesitation and ask the stupidest question! With this idea in mind, here we list down few of the societies which can become your saviour at times. We highly recommend you joining these and find a buddy in your journey. Here we go!

Essential developer communities

  1. The SDESkills Discord Community

    2. I highly recommend joining Discord as there are some awesome communities' servers hosted on Discord in a well-organised manner. There are different channels for different topics making it really easy to float your queries. Head over to this to know more on SDESkills Discord community.

  2. Code NewBie

    3. The name says it all! It's an awesome community to connect with when you are a newbie to all of this. They have a slack community and you are invited to make the best use of it in your personalised manner. They have also joined hands with DEV to organise a community-first conference, as they say, to level up your journey.

  3. Women Who Code

  4. Coursera Communities

  5. ML India

    6. Not a community like the above listed ones but an instagram page with awesome quality content. One should definitely check it out if they haven't done yet.

Agile methodology:

Git: Git is a version control system that tracks changes to files in a project over time. It typically records what the changes were (what was added? what was removed from the file?), who made the changes, notes and comments about the changes by the changer, and at what time the changes were made. It is primarily used for software development projects which are often collaborative, so in this sense, it is a tool to help enable and improve collaboration.

Documentation: As Google says - "official information". So, basically it is a collection of all the arguments that a function can take or of all the commands/functions that a library consists of.
