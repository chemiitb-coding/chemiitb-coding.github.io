NetBeans, VS Code, Eclipse or what?

Why emphasize the environment?

By emphasizing, the focus is not on giving preference to a specific IDE (Integrated Development Environment) but on getting started with the IDEs. It is very obvious at the beginning to scratch your head getting started with coding, not wrt the language but wrt the platform to use and how to actually get your codes running. Every other person you talk will list down infi many cons of Windows and pros of Ubuntu but you don't know what exactly are you supposed to do. That's quite normal. Let's take it from the beginning :)

What exactly is an IDE?

IDE stands for Integrated Development Environment. Quite a big word no? Let's break it down. When you write a code somewhere on your device, it's as equal as writing it with pen on a piece of paper. It actually comes to life when it gets executed. This execution thing is the role of a software which we call as 'compiler'.
